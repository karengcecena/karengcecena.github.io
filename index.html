ABOUT

I'm a small-town desert girl turned Software Engineer with a passion and determination to find new ways to use tech for social good, improve diversity and #changetheratio in tech, and incorporate tech opportunities for minority populations.

Growing up, when I wasn’t outside running around and adventuring in the desert with my friends, you would find me solving puzzles (sudokus mainly), reading, learning, snapping pictures and videos, and re-watching movies & tv shows (we all know they don’t make rom-coms like they did in the late 90s/early 2000s).

Now, you can find me doing the same, except instead of getting my daily fix of problem solving through sudokus, I get it through coding! With a history in health care, I have always found joy in communicating with and helping others. However, I often found myself craving a bigger challenge.

During my last quarter of undergrad at the University of California, San Diego, I had the opportunity of taking a Microbiology Lab where the command line was introduced to me. After that, I dipped my toes in an Intro to Python course, confirmed my love for programming, attended Hackbrights Software Engineering Bootcamp, and have never looked back! I absolutely love that coding tickles my problem solving brain in the same place that sudokus and The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild do.
